# Capital Budgeting & Investment Analysis Tool

## Project Overview

This project presents an Excel-based capital budgeting and investment analysis model created as part of a business finance game.

The main objective of the project was to complete the missing yellow sections in the workbook by using the information provided in the white input tables. The model was built to calculate pricing, profitability, cash flows, IRR, WACC and Net Present Value in order to evaluate whether the investment/project met the required financial targets.

## Key Features

- Pricing model based on cost, margin and service tower targets
- Year-by-year revenue and cost breakdown
- Gross Profit and Gross Profit Margin calculation
- Price adjustment to meet the required total target price
- Client-view pricing split across five years
- Income and Expense Statement
- Cash Flow Statement
- Pre-Tax Income and PTI Margin calculation
- Corporate tax calculation
- Net Income calculation
- IRR calculation
- WACC calculation using cost of equity, cost of debt and capital structure
- NPV calculation using discounted project cash flows
- Comparison of project results against financial targets

## Financial Concepts Covered

### Capital Budgeting

Capital budgeting is used to evaluate whether a project or investment is financially attractive.

In this model, the project is analyzed by forecasting revenues, costs, profits and cash flows over a five-year period.

### Pricing Analysis

The pricing section calculates the selling price required to cover project costs while achieving the required Gross Profit percentages.

The model separates the project into different service towers, including consulting, managed services and third-party software.

### Gross Profit

Gross Profit is calculated as revenue minus cost.

The model also calculates Gross Profit Margin to check whether each service tower meets the required profitability targets.

### Price Adjustment

The project required the final total price to be close to a target price of 45 million.

A price adjustment section was used to allocate markup across the service towers and years so that the final client-view price matched the required total project price.

### Income and Expense Statement

The Income and Expense Statement shows annual revenue, cost, gross profit, expenses, pre-tax income, corporate tax and net income.

This section helps evaluate the accounting profitability of the project.

### Cash Flow Statement

The Cash Flow Statement tracks cash inflows and cash outflows during each year of the project.

Net cash flow is then used to calculate investment return metrics such as IRR and NPV.

### IRR

Internal Rate of Return measures the project’s expected annualized return based on its cash flows.

The model compares the calculated IRR against the required target IRR to determine whether the project meets the investment hurdle rate.

### WACC

Weighted Average Cost of Capital is calculated using the cost of equity, cost of debt, tax rate and capital structure.

WACC is used as the discount rate for the NPV calculation.

### Net Present Value

NPV discounts the project’s future net cash flows using the calculated WACC.

A positive NPV indicates that the project is expected to create value after accounting for the required return.

## Main Insights

The project shows how pricing decisions directly affect profitability, cash flow and investment value.

The final pricing model was adjusted to reach the required total target price while still meeting the required Gross Profit percentages for the main service towers.

The financial analysis showed whether the project met key investment targets such as PTI margin, IRR and positive NPV.

The model also demonstrates how WACC is used as a discount rate to calculate NPV and evaluate whether a project creates value.
